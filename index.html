Reasons for population decrease:

Suburbanization

Following World War II, people began leaving cities for the new housing found in suburbs. The construction of the Interstate System allowed people to easily commute to their city jobs from their new homes.

Deindustrialization

The decline of the industrial sector led to severe population loss, as well as economic decline. Industries that saw major decline following World War II include steel (affecting Pittsburgh and Columbus, among others) and the auto industry (affecting Detroit).

Reasons for population increase:

Locations of Growing Industries

Many cities experiencing exponential growth are homes to industries that have also seen much growth in recent years, including the technology industry and the banking industry.
